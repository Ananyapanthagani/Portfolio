Pet Valu Mobile
UX Redesign

A self-directed concept redesign of Pet Valu's mobile login and checkout experience — built on real customer research, solving real user pain points, within real brand constraints.

The Problem

Pet Valu's online customers were frustrated and leaving.

Pet Valu is one of Canada's largest pet specialty retailers with 800+ stores nationwide. But their online experience was letting down their most loyal customers — the Devoted Pet Lovers (DPL).

Before designing anything, I spent time on petvalu.ca on mobile and researched real customer reviews on Trustpilot and the Better Business Bureau. Three critical pain points emerged consistently across hundreds of reviews.

01

Repeated Logouts

Customers reported being unexpectedly logged out mid-session, forcing them to reset passwords and restart their shopping journey entirely.

02

Checkout Confusion

Users had no visibility into where they were in the checkout process, leading to abandoned carts and repeated payment attempts.

03

Vague Delivery Info

Shipping was described as "5-7 business days" with no concrete date — causing frustration, complaints, and trust erosion with loyal customers.

Research & Insights

What real customers were saying.

I analyzed reviews across multiple platforms to understand the real emotional experience of Pet Valu's online customers. The feedback was consistent — the website was breaking trust with the very customers who loved the brand most.

🔐

"I keep getting logged out and have to reset my password every time"

Login session management was failing, creating repeated friction for returning customers who expected a seamless experience.

→ Design fix: Persistent "Keep me signed in" toggle
💳

"The payment page kept glitching and I didn't know if my order went through"

Payment errors showed generic messages that gave no actionable guidance, causing customers to attempt multiple charges.

→ Design fix: Clear, friendly error messaging with specific guidance
📦

"Shipping took nearly two weeks with no updates"

Vague shipping windows created anxiety and eroded trust. Customers felt uninformed and unable to plan around their orders.

→ Design fix: Exact delivery date shown at checkout ("Arrives by April 8")
👤

"It kept forcing me to check out as a guest"

The account vs guest flow was confusing, making loyal customers feel like the platform didn't recognize or value them.

→ Design fix: Guest as secondary option, account creation framed as rewarding
The Designs

3 screens. 3 real problems solved.

I designed a complete mobile-first Login, Checkout, and Payment flow in Figma — addressing every pain point identified in research, within Pet Valu's existing brand guidelines and with AODA accessibility compliance throughout.

Design Decisions

Every choice had a reason.

Each design decision was directly connected to a real user complaint or business requirement from the Pet Valu job description.

Screen 1 — Login

"Keep me signed in" Toggle

Directly addresses the #1 complaint — users being logged out unexpectedly. A simple toggle gives users control and immediately builds trust back into the experience.

Screen 1 — Login

Guest as Secondary Option

"Continue as Guest" is visible but not the primary CTA. Account creation is framed as valuable: "Create an account and earn rewards" — turning a chore into a benefit.

Screen 2 — Checkout

4-Step Progress Indicator

Cart → Delivery → Payment → Confirm. Users always know exactly where they are and how far they have to go — eliminating the anxiety of an unclear checkout process.

Screen 2 — Checkout

Exact Delivery Date

"Arrives by Wednesday April 8" instead of "ships in 5-7 business days." A concrete date sets clear expectations and removes the anxiety of vague shipping windows.

Screen 3 — Payment

Security Trust Badge

A subtle "Secure Checkout — Your payment info is encrypted" banner directly addresses payment trust issues raised in customer reviews, reducing checkout abandonment.

Screen 3 — Payment

Friendly Error State

"Your card was declined — please check your details" in plain language. Not a generic error code. Tells users exactly what went wrong and what to do next.

What I Learned

Key Takeaways

Research before design

Reading real customer reviews before opening Figma completely changed what I designed. Every screen decision came from a real complaint — not just what looked good.

Constraints are creative

Working within Pet Valu's brand guidelines — keeping their logo, their red, their style — pushed me to be more creative with UX improvements rather than visual ones.

Error states matter

Most designers only design the happy path. Designing what happens when something goes wrong — and making it friendly — is where real UX value is created.
